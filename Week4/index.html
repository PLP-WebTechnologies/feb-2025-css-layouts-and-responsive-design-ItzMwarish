China's Authoritarian Growth

  • 2008
    • Infrastructure Boom Begins: Beijing Olympics catalyzes massive development—stadiums, roads, and airports.
      Launch of large-scale urban rail networks and high-speed rail (HSR) planning.
  • 2010
    • World's Longest High-Speed Rail Network: China opens 8,358 km of high-speed rail.
      By 2023, the network exceeds 42,000 km — more than the rest of the world combined.
  • 2013
    • Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) Launched: Aims to boost global trade routes through infrastructure across Asia, Africa, and Europe.
      Includes construction of ports, railways, and roads abroad.
  • 2015
    • Tech Leap: Rise of Huawei & Alibaba.
      Huawei becomes a global leader in 5G technology.
      Alibaba and Tencent revolutionize mobile payments and e-commerce.
      Cashless economy takes off via WeChat Pay and Alipay.
  • 2017
    • Artificial Intelligence Development Plan: Government sets national goal to be world AI leader by 2030.
      Heavy investment in facial recognition, surveillance tech, and smart cities.
  • 2020
    • Economic Resilience During COVID: One of the only major economies to grow in 2020.
      Swift lockdowns and state control keep factories running and exports strong.
  • 2021
    • Space & Tech Sovereignty: Launches Tianhe space station module, aiming to rival ISS.
      Expands tech independence amid global sanctions.
  • 2023
    • Digital Yuan Pilots Nationwide: China becomes the first major power to officially pilot a central bank digital currency (CBDC) at scale.
